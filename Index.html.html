Highlights included

Bank of England gold bar, Greenwich, Mudchute Farm, Crossrail Roof Garden, practice golf, Stonehenge, castles, and a compact Paris trip.

Style of trip

Comfortable pace, geography clustered where possible, with room for family visits, weather changes, and rest days.

Week 1 · London orientation

Arrival week: keep it impressive, flexible, and not too tiring.

Day 1

Arrival + South Bank evening

Low effort River walk Great first impression
  • Settle in, refresh, and keep the afternoon easy.
  • Walk Westminster Bridge, the London Eye stretch, and South Bank.
  • Dinner with a river view or somewhere casual nearby.
Day 2

Westminster walking day

Classic London Good for first-timers
  • Westminster, Big Ben, Westminster Abbey, Buckingham Palace area.
  • Optional guided walking tour for easy context and storytelling.
  • Covent Garden later for food, performers, and browsing.
Day 3

Tower of London + Tower Bridge

History Family friendly
  • Do the Tower of London and allow time for the Beefeater tour.
  • Walk Tower Bridge and the riverside around St Katharine Docks.
  • Optional Sky Garden later if energy allows.
Days 4–7

Museum, quirky London, park time, and family visits

Flexible Weather backup built in
  • British Museum or Natural History Museum.
  • The Clink Prison Museum or Borough Market for something more unusual.
  • Hyde Park / Kensington Gardens for a slower day.
  • Use one day as a family or catch-up buffer.

Week 2 · East London, day trips, and standout experiences

This is the most varied week: finance, Greenwich, modern London, and a bigger excursion day.

Day 8

Bank of England + City of London

Unique Gold bar experience
  • Visit the Bank of England Museum and try the gold bar lift.
  • Walk the City afterwards for the old-meets-modern London contrast.
  • Option to combine with St Paul’s or Leadenhall Market.
Bank of England Museum
Day 9

Greenwich full day

One of the best all-round days Adult + kid friendly
  • National Maritime Museum.
  • Greenwich Market for lunch.
  • Royal Observatory and the university / Old Royal Naval College area.
  • Optional Thames boat one way for extra fun.
Maritime Museum Greenwich Market Royal Observatory Old Royal Naval College
Day 10

Canary Wharf + Mudchute

Modern London Easy walking
  • Crossrail Place Roof Garden / Canary Wharf wander.
  • Coffee and dockside time.
  • Mudchute Farm for a surprising London contrast.
Crossrail Roof Garden Mudchute Farm
Day 11

North Greenwich practice golf

Activity day Low pressure
  • Driving range or golf practice session.
  • Possible O2 / riverside walk afterwards.
  • Good light day after more intense sightseeing.
Greenwich Peninsula Golf
Day 12

Stonehenge day

Big excursion Best with early start
  • Go as a guided day trip or self-drive.
  • Option to pair with Bath if you want a full classic day out.
Stonehenge
Day 13

Castle day

Choose one Windsor or Warwick
  • Windsor Castle: easier, iconic, royal.
  • Warwick Castle: more theatrical and dramatic.
Windsor Castle Warwick Castle

Paris add-on · 2 to 3 days

Compact and high-impact. Enough to see the essentials without turning it into a marathon.

Paris Day 1

Arrival + Eiffel Tower + Seine

Classic Best first evening
  • Take Eurostar from London.
  • Check in, then Eiffel Tower area and a Seine cruise or river walk.
Eurostar Eiffel Tower
Paris Day 2

Louvre + Notre-Dame + Champs-Élysées

Big icons Pick your pace
  • Do not try to cover the whole Louvre. Choose a focused route.
  • Walk through the central historic area afterwards.
Louvre Notre-Dame
Optional Paris Day 3

Montmartre + Sacré-Cœur + relaxed return

Slower pace Best if staying 3 days
  • A more atmospheric finish rather than another heavy attraction day.
  • Then head back to London without feeling rushed.

Week 3 · Buffer, family time, and optional overnight trip

Keep the final stretch lighter so the trip still feels enjoyable by the end.

Option A

Stay London-based and keep it easy

Best for lower stress Flexible
  • Shopping, local neighbourhood time, one final museum or market, and family days.
  • Use this week for anything missed because of weather or availability.
Option B

One short overnight trip

Good if everyone still has energy Wales works better than Scotland
  • Snowdonia / Eryri is the more practical scenic option if you want one hike-based trip.
  • Better suited than Ben Nevis for a short add-on within this London-centred holiday.
